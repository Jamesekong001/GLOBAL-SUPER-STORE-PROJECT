# GLOBAL-SUPER-STORE-
Global Super Store Sales Dashboard analyzes sales, profit, quantity, and shipping cost across regions, cities, and product categories, providing actionable insights for business performance optimization and data-driven decision-making.
Global Super Store Analysis Dashboard
# Project Overview
This project presents an interactive Global Super Store Sales Dashboard designed to analyze key business performance metrics including sales, profit, quantity sold, and shipping cost across different regions, cities, and customer segments. The dashboard provides actionable insights to support strategic decision-making.
# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
• Total Sales: ₦12,642,905.00
• Total Quantity Sold: 178,312 units
• Total Profit: ₦1,464,757.29
• Total Shipping Cost: ₦1,352,815.70
# Sales Performance Insights
1. Sales by Sub-Category
Phones generated the highest sales, followed by Chairs and Copiers. Storage recorded the lowest sales among the displayed categories. This indicates Phones are the top-performing product line.
2. Quantity Sold by City
New York City recorded the highest quantity sold, followed by Los Angeles. Smaller cities such as Managua and Tegucigalpa recorded lower volumes.
3. Profit Trend by Year
Profit increased consistently from 2011 to 2014:
2011: ₦248,940.81
2012: ₦307,415.28
2013: ₦406,935.23
2014: ₦504,165.97
This indicates steady business growth.
4. Shipping Cost by Ship Mode
Standard Class accounts for the largest shipping cost, followed by Second Class. Same Day delivery contributes the least.
5. Region by Profit
The Central region generated the highest profit, followed by North and South regions. Some regions such as Caribbean and Canada recorded lower profitability.
6. Country by Shipping Cost
The United States recorded the highest shipping cost, followed by Australia and France.
# Business Insights & Recommendations
• Focus on high-performing product categories (Phones, Chairs).
• Expand market penetration in top-performing cities.
• Maintain growth momentum in strong regions.
• Optimize shipping operations in high-cost countries.
• Improve efficiency in low-performing regions.
